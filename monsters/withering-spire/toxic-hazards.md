# Toxic Hazards

The Withering Spire is soaked in poison before you ever meet a monster. Scattered through its rooms are several flavors of standing hazard — none of them fightable, all of them worth reading before you walk through a room at half health.

**Found in:** [Withering Spire](/dungeons/withering-spire) — throughout the dungeon

| Stat | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Health | Unkillable — invisible, immovable triggers |
| Damage | Varies (poison-based; see below) |
| Threat | Environmental hazard |

## Withering Bog

Invisible ground hazards that periodically erupt in a jet of noxious gas, poisoning anyone standing in the burst radius. Comes in three sizes (Small and Medium are placed directly through the dungeon; the Large size is exclusively summoned by [Verdelith](/monsters/withering-spire/verdelith)'s Bog Slam in her third phase) — bigger bogs hit a wider radius and poison for longer.

## Toxic Gas Vents

Armor-stand emitters that periodically fill an area with rising green fog, poisoning and disorienting (nausea) anyone caught in it. Small and Medium vents are common throughout the Spire; a Large size is defined in the dungeon's data but wasn't found wired to any actual spawn point.

## Miasma Fissure & Geyser

A bigger, telegraphed version of the gas vents — a warning message ("A fissure splits the ground nearby...") gives you a moment before it erupts in an escalating column of gas that poisons and disorients out to a wide radius. In practice the only confirmed source is [Verdelith](/monsters/withering-spire/verdelith)'s Acid Slam, which tears open a Miasma Geyser during her second phase; the Miasma Fissure variant exists in the data but doesn't appear to be placed anywhere in the current dungeon.

## Toxic Spout

A recurring ground vent that hisses, then fires a poison bolt straight up before raining lingering poison particles back down — one of the more common standalone hazards in the dungeon. Standing on top of it when it fires costs you 8 damage and poison; the falling residue keeps poisoning anyone who stays close afterward.

## Soul Reaper Vines

A buried trap disguised as a patch of roots — indistinguishable from decoration until you step within a block of it. It lashes up out of the ground for 3 damage and poison, then resets to trigger again. One of the more common hazards in the Spire — watch your footing in overgrown rooms.

## Acid Pool

Not a standalone hazard you'll find placed on its own — this is the lingering poison puddle left behind by [Volatile Sludge](/monsters/withering-spire/volatile-sludge) deaths and dragged along [Bilepod](/monsters/withering-spire/aldric-fennwick)'s Lurch charge. Standing in one costs repeated poison ticks plus small direct damage.

## Drops

None from any of these — they're triggers and emitters, not monsters.
